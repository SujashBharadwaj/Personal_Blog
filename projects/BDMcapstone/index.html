Introduction to the BDM Capstone Project

About BDM Capstone Project. The capstone is an independent research project. A student connects with a real business in an organized sector like manufacturing, IT, or automobile or in an unorganized sector like kirana stores and vendors. The student identifies a business problem, collects primary data, cleans it, analyzes it, and presents useful insights to decision makers.

Purpose of the course. The BDM theory course is a prerequisite. Data drives decisions, but the reasoning and reporting must be clear. This project trains you to justify your approach, link sections into a professional document, and show how insights help decision makers. It is hands on from raw data to a recommendation.

Project stages and weightage

  • Proposal. 4 to 5 pages. Organization background, problem statement and context, approach with justification, timeline with WBS and Gantt, expected outcomes. Weightage 20 percent.
  • Mid term. 8 to 10 pages. Short video proof with the business owner or manager, field notes, metadata, descriptive statistics, and preliminary analysis with interpretation. Weightage 20 percent.
  • Final report. 18 to 20 pages. Complete end to end report with methods, results, and recommendations. Weightage 40 percent.
  • Viva voce. Up to 10 slides to present and defend the project. Weightage 20 percent.

After all four stages, top projects are shortlisted and showcased to industry professionals.
